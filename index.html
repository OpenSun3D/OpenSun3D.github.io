Introduction

For intelligent agents to thrive in the physical world, they must not only perceive but also comprehend and act within it. This year’s workshop unites leading researchers advancing world models and spatial intelligence, from 3D perception and scene understanding to generative world representations. Through keynotes and a challenge on open 3D interaction perception, we will explore how machines learn to reason about and engage with their environments. The workshop aims to shape the next generation of spatially grounded AI, bridging scientific discovery, practical deployment, and responsible innovation.

Huawei SpatialMem Track

Sponsored by

A dedicated sub-track on memory-centric, online, dynamic/4D or world-model-coupled spatial intelligence. Includes both archival and non-archival submissions. Authors may submit to the main workshop track and also flag their paper as SpatialMem for consideration in this sub-track. Submit as proceedings or non-archival exactly as above and simply flag your paper as SpatialMem at submission.

🏆 Best Paper Award & a dedicated Best SpatialMem Paper Award funded by Huawei.
Topics of interest ▾
  • Dynamic & Hierarchical Spatial Representations — persistent open-vocabulary 3D scene graphs (metric, semantic, topological, 4D) and multi-view-consistent, metric-grounded VLM reasoning.
  • Test-Time Memory Evolution & Updating — updating, editing and bounding a 3D memory at inference across revisits; consolidation, selective forgetting, and memory for video world models.
  • Neuro-Symbolic Spatial Reasoning — verifiable programs, tools and graph-query interfaces over 3D scene graphs for checkable inference.
  • Systems & Efficiency — on-device, power/memory-budgeted mapping + reasoning; constant-memory streaming maps and deployment-overhead benchmarks.
  • Multi-Session Persistent Spatial Memory — representations and benchmarks for memory persisting across sessions (hours to days+), tracking identity, relations, uncertainty and provenance.

Challenge Track Open

We host a 3-track challenge based on the SceneFun3D benchmark and the Articulate3D dataset. The challenge focuses on fine-grained functionality, affordance, and interaction understanding in 3D indoor environments.
